Overview

Aim

To create a better experience for accessing legal information and conducting research for legal professionals.


Retrieving information is crucial as it helps legal professionals support their legal decision-making process. Hence, there is a need to carry out proper legal research effectively in a user-friendly manner. The information retrieved must be easily usable for cases, projects, etc. and hence there requires this void to be filled.

My Design Process

Understand

Survey

To better understand my customer target audience, I had conducted a survey with the participants who are related to the law industry. The survey was structured in a way where the aim was to understand customer motivations, goals and current behaviours/patterns on how their approach towards research is currently taking place.

Therefore, my target users would be legal students and professionals who are dependent on legal research in their daily work.

This process has been insightful in understand what my users really need and what problems they are currently facing, and has thus helped me in my Competitive Analysis with some of the legal apps.



Competitive Analysis

After conducting a heuristic evaluation, and understanding these apps better, I mapped out painpoints and opportunity areas in terms of "How Might We's"


Paint-Points I found:

  1. The platforms are not user-friendly - Poor legibility and have bad interface
  2. Poor consistency and hard to navigate within the app
  3. Time-consuming
  4. The experience feels overwhelming

Opportunity Areas in terms of "How Might We's":

  1. How Might We make the experience quick and accurate?
  2. How Might We make the experience feel more user-friendly and consistent?
  3. How Might We increase the no. of people using smartphones for legal research?
  4. How Might We make working in projects seem less overwhelming through Share, Save and Print?

Mapping Out Findings

Based on my above insights, I then started mapping out to conceptualise on my opportunity areas and give it some form in terms of a product and how the experience could possibly run. This has helped me map about the experience and also the product structure.

My Learnings

Reflection

  • The process could have been more efficient if we had tested and validated at every stage with our target audience.
  • I realised the importance of understanding behaviors and motivations in people.
  • I learnt that understanding the brand and the service's goals to be crucial in the design process. In a way, this helps shape the product in a much more functional manner that sticks with our customer audience.
  • In terms of the product features, there was still a lot of features to be explored and a heuristic analysis/usability testing would help us take a better decision in our decision process.
  • The design was minimal initially but the aim was to remain in the intersection b/w replicating something that our target audience is already comforable with and the brand's character.
